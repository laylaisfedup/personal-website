Progress

Thoughts on how we make things better.

Why Progress Matters

Progress isn't inevitable. It requires intention, effort, and the right conditions. Understanding what enables progress helps us create more of it.

Historical Examples

Cases of rapid progress worth studying:

  • The Apollo Program (1961-1969) - From Kennedy's speech to the moon in 8 years
  • The Manhattan Project (1942-1945) - Unprecedented coordination of science and engineering
  • The Green Revolution (1960s-1970s) - Agricultural innovations that fed billions
  • mRNA Vaccines (2020) - Decades of research enabling rapid pandemic response

What Enables Progress?

  • Urgency - Clear deadlines and high stakes focus effort
  • Talent density - Great people attract and elevate each other
  • Resources - Adequate funding without bureaucratic overhead
  • Autonomy - Freedom to pursue unconventional approaches
  • Feedback loops - Rapid iteration and learning

What Inhibits Progress?

  • Excessive regulation and compliance burden
  • Short-term thinking in funding and incentives
  • Risk aversion in institutions
  • Coordination failures between disciplines
  • Loss of ambition and belief that progress is possible

Questions I'm Exploring

  • Why did progress seem faster in some eras?
  • What institutional forms best support innovation?
  • How do we balance caution with ambition?
  • Can we deliberately create conditions for breakthroughs?
